The Honest Case for Switching from Cardboard

Cardboard produce boxes are cheap per unit and widely available. There's a reason they've been the default for decades. But at scale, the economics shift significantly.

A produce distributor moving 10,000 boxes per month spends $10,000–$30,000 annually on cardboard — and disposes of every single box after one use. Wet cold chain environments accelerate cardboard failure, leading to product damage and contamination risk.

PP corrugated boxes from our top-rated manufacturer are reusable for 100+ cycles, fully waterproof, washable to food-grade standards, and 100% recyclable at end of life. The per-use cost after the first cycle is a fraction of cardboard. For operations at scale, the ROI case is clear.
